# CORI Alpha Proof Chain

**Release family:** CRK-1 companion specification  
**Status:** Frozen  
**Depends on:** CIS Core v1.0  
**Stewardship:** CIS release maintainers  
**Constitutional sections:** 1-6  
**Informative sections:** 7

---

## 1. Purpose

CORI Alpha is the first constitutional proof milestone for the CIS ecosystem.

This specification defines the minimum governed workflow that must be demonstrated before CORI Alpha may be considered a valid constitutional proof.
It is the first independently verifiable constitutional proof in the release family.

## 2. Minimum proof workflow

The CORI Alpha proof chain SHALL demonstrate, in order:

- Identity
- Evidence
- Provenance
- Trust
- Relationships
- Constitutional Decision
- Constitutional Receipt
- Replay
- Conformance
- Evidence Package

The proof chain SHALL be end-to-end replayable and anchored to the frozen CIS Core baseline.
The proof chain SHALL also remain traceable through the frozen GKS, Research OS, conformance suite, launch-readiness, and SOCK alignment baselines.

## 3. Proof requirements

Each step in the proof chain SHALL be:

- independently reviewable
- traceable to CIS Core
- linked to the governing companion specification
- backed by evidence artifacts

Each step SHALL preserve a constitutional receipt or traceable equivalent where receipts are not yet available.
Each step SHALL be expressible as evidence that an independent steward can review without private explanation.

## 4. Execution path

The CORI Alpha execution path SHALL consume evidence from the following frozen companion baselines:

- Governed Knowledge Store Specification
- Research OS Specification
- CIS Conformance Suite Specification
- CIS Launch Readiness Specification
- SOCK Alignment Specification

Each baseline SHALL contribute a distinct evidence surface to the proof chain:

- GKS provides canonical knowledge admission, canonical state, and supersession continuity.
- Research OS provides the Evidence -> Insights -> Ideas -> Actions improvement loop.
- The conformance suite provides constitutional, replay, receipt, trust, and acceptance validation.
- Launch readiness provides evidence-based promotion gates for the release boundary.
- SOCK alignment provides the runtime bridge between constitutional obligations and execution semantics.

## 5. Constitutional decision

The constitutional decision SHALL record:

- the governing requirement
- the steward or stewarding body
- the decision outcome
- the evidence basis
- the replay reference

The decision record SHALL identify whether the proof chain satisfies the minimum governed workflow or remains partial.

## 6. Evidence package

The evidence package SHALL include at minimum:

- the identity record
- the evidence set
- the provenance record
- the trust ledger reference
- the relationship graph
- the constitutional decision record
- the constitutional receipt
- the replay artifact or replay path
- the conformance result

The evidence package SHALL be sufficient for an independent steward to review the proof chain without private explanation.
The evidence package SHALL be sufficient for replay, trust validation, receipt validation, and constitutional verification.

## 7. Conformance

CORI Alpha SHALL be considered proven only when the proof chain is replayable and the evidence package demonstrates conformance to the frozen constitutional baseline.
CORI Alpha SHALL not be marked proven until the conformance suite and launch-readiness gates both pass with objective evidence.

## 8. Informative note

CORI Alpha is not a feature-count milestone.
It is a governed proof milestone.
