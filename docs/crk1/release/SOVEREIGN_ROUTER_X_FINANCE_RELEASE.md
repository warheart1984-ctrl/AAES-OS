# Sovereign Router X Finance Release

**Release family:** CRK-1 companion specification  
**Purpose:** Explain target margin bands, packaging strategy, and segment-level commercial posture for Sovereign Router X.

---

## 1. Finance posture

Sovereign Router X is priced as a constitutional orchestration platform with multiple value drivers.

Finance should evaluate the product on:

- recurring platform subscription revenue
- routed usage revenue
- governance and assurance revenue
- knowledge services revenue
- enterprise deployment revenue
- professional services revenue

The goal is not to mark up inference alone. The goal is to preserve durable margin through packaging, governance, and continuity services.

## 2. Segment targets

| Segment | Preferred commercial shape | Indicative target margin band | Packaging strategy |
|---------|----------------------------|-------------------------------|--------------------|
| Individual | Subscription-led | 55-70% | Low-entry plan, simple billing, minimal support burden |
| Professional | Subscription-led or hybrid | 60-75% | Tiered workspace with usage expansion and optional assurance |
| Team | Subscription-led with assurance attach | 62-78% | Seat-based pricing with shared admin, evidence, and workflow controls |
| Enterprise | Enterprise bundle | 70-85% | Annual commitment, private deployment, assurance, support, and services |
| Public Sector | Enterprise bundle with procurement controls | 65-80% | Audit-friendly packaging, private routing, evidence retention, and contract clarity |

## 3. Packaging strategy by model

### Subscription-led

Best for:

- Individual
- Professional
- Team

Finance rationale:

- predictable recurring revenue
- lower billing complexity
- easier gross margin planning

### Usage-led

Best for:

- Professional
- Team
- variable Enterprise workloads

Finance rationale:

- aligns price to volume
- protects against underpriced heavy usage
- requires strict metering and overage controls

### Assurance-led

Best for:

- Team
- Enterprise
- Public Sector

Finance rationale:

- monetizes evidence, audit, and policy work
- improves margin where governance is a customer requirement
- reduces dependence on inference economics

### Enterprise bundle

Best for:

- Enterprise
- Public Sector

Finance rationale:

- supports private deployment and high-touch procurement
- consolidates multiple value drivers into one contract
- strongest route for resilient account economics

## 4. Margin guardrails

Recommended planning guardrails:

- keep standard-tier direct inference cost below 35 percent of revenue
- keep orchestration, storage, and replay below 10 percent of revenue
- price assurance as a durable value layer, not a discounted add-on
- require enterprise bundles to carry support and deployment costs explicitly
- validate margin by segment before approving any packaging change

## 5. Finance review checklist

Before approving pricing changes, confirm:

- segment mix assumptions
- routed volume assumptions
- support load assumptions
- assurance workload assumptions
- private deployment cost assumptions
- services utilization assumptions
- contract length and renewal assumptions
