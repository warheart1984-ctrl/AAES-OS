# Research OS Specification

**Release family:** CRK-1 companion specification  
**Status:** Frozen  
**Depends on:** CIS Core v1.0  
**Stewardship:** CIS release maintainers  
**Constitutional sections:** 1-6  
**Informative sections:** 7

---

## 1. Purpose

Research OS is the ecosystem's durable research operating layer.

Its purpose is to preserve research as a governed, reusable workflow rather than as isolated notes.

Research OS SHALL remain a single-responsibility companion specification for evidence stewardship and improvement loops.

Research OS SHALL inherit the governed knowledge model established by GKS.

## 2. Research workflow

Research OS SHALL use the following workflow:

Evidence -> Insights -> Ideas -> Actions

Each stage SHALL preserve traceability to the prior stage.

## 3. Research objects

Research OS SHALL preserve at minimum:

- Evidence
- Insights
- Ideas
- Actions

Each object SHALL include traceability metadata sufficient for review and reuse.

## 4. Constitutional role

Research OS SHALL inherit CIS Core terminology.

Research OS SHALL NOT redefine constitutional terms.

Research OS SHALL support the constitutional baseline by turning research output into stewarded improvements.

## 5. Outputs

Research OS SHOULD produce:

- evidence packages
- review notes
- improvement candidates
- action records
- traceable references into companion specifications

Each output SHOULD be linkable back to the original evidence set and forward to a concrete action.

## 6. Conformance

A conforming Research OS implementation SHALL:

- preserve the Evidence -> Insights -> Ideas -> Actions workflow
- maintain durable traceability between research objects
- keep research outputs linked to CIS Core and the traceability matrix
- support review without rewriting the constitutional baseline

Conformance SHALL be demonstrable through replayable evidence and stable object lineage.

Research OS is part of the frozen companion baseline for CIS Core v1.0.

## 7. Informative note

Research OS is the institutional memory and improvement loop for the CIS ecosystem.
