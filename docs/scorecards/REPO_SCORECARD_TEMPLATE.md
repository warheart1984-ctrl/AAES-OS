## Constitutional Ontology

Use these definitions consistently across the repository:

- Authority: legitimate standing to authorize an action or obligation
- Evidence: information that supports or challenges a claim independently of who presents it
- Verification: the process of testing whether claims, evidence, or implementations satisfy defined criteria
- Compliance: conformance to constitutional obligations, policies, or authorized constraints
- Truth: independent reality, regardless of authority, evidence, verification, or compliance

These five concepts must remain constitutionally distinct.

## Canonical Replay & Evidence Contract (CREC)

Every repository in the constitutional computing stack must expose the same contract surface, even when the underlying implementation remains independent.

### Required Fields

- Intent
- Authority
- Evidence
- Verification
- Compliance
- Truth Boundary
- Replay Record
- Audit Trail
- Failure Path
- Proof & Challenge Surface
- Failure Contracts
- Observability
- Proof Surface Level (P0-P5)
- Constitutional Maturity
- Commercial Readiness

### CREC Rules

- Intent must state what the repository is trying to prove or enable.
- Authority must identify the governing source for the claim.
- Evidence must list the artifacts that support the claim.
- Verification must describe how the evidence is independently checked.
- Compliance must name the applicable constitutional or repository rules.
- Truth Boundary must say what the repository does not prove.
- Replay Record must identify the path to reconstruct the claim.
- Audit Trail must identify the path to inspect the claim after the fact.
- Failure Path must explain what happens when evidence is missing or invalid.
- Proof & Challenge Surface must identify the evidence that supports a claim and the evidence that could invalidate it.
- Failure Contracts must define expected failures, unexpected failures, degradation behavior, safe fallback, quarantine conditions, and recovery path.
- Observability must define metrics, traces, evidence, replay, proof surface, and constitutional health.
- Proof Surface Level must declare the highest supported level from P0 to P5.
- Constitutional Maturity must state the present constitutional state, not the aspirational one.
- Commercial Readiness must state the present commercialization state, not the market promise.

### CREC Rule

No repository may claim a higher constitutional state than its current evidence supports.
